Search and Rescue Robot

The final group project of "MAE 322: Mechanical Design" is to design and manufacture a Search and Rescue Robot (SaRR). I was enrolled in this course in Fall 2024. The robot traversed a course that included going over a ramp, traveling through a chute, ascending a foot high wall, and delivering a medical kit into a basket. I was responsible for coding and manufacturing the SaRR. This course/project is curremtly in progress.

The robot is 16" x 24" x 10" with four proximity sensors and two cadmium sulfide light sensors. Our design choices were based largely on goals for consistent driveability and mounting the wall obstacle. Two different types of wheels will be utilized: the front wheels will be omni-directional wheels and the back wheels will be traction wheels. The motors will be connected to the wheels via a pulley and timing belt system. The frame of the robot is made out of 6061 aluminim for its high strength and relatively low weight.
